Codecember 2019: Patterns

I cannot emphasize enough how much progress this work is in.

This project is heavily inspired by a Japanese book of patterns from 1824 by Hokusai (he made The Great Wave off Kanagawa) (that one Japanese painting you know about) (with the wave). The book is called 新形小紋帳. I'm not sure what it means but it likely involves the words “new”, “pattern”, and “komon” (which is a type of kimono). An Edo komon is a komon with a small repeating pattern, which checks out.

I found out about this book through a tweet by a stationary store in London(???) and I found out about its origin and purpose through this post. The book is available online and apparently The British Museum has a copy. The Sumida Hokusai Museum in Tokyo probably also has a copy and that copy is in a cooler looking building.
